Best places for trekking ;


Way to Ladakh
Ladakh, in Jammu and Kashmir, is a sought-after destination for trekking. With stunning landscapes, high passes, and ancient monasteries, it offers thrilling routes like Markha Valley and Chadar Trek. Trekking in Ladakh allows you to immerse in its unique culture, witness breathtaking scenery of mountains and lakes, making it an unforgettable adventure in the Himalayas.



Way to Manali
Manali, located in the Indian state of Himachal Pradesh, offers breathtaking trekking opportunities for adventure enthusiasts. The region is known for its stunning landscapes, snow-capped mountains, and serene valleys. Trekking in Manali allows you to explore the Himalayan wilderness and witness its natural beauty up close. Popular treks in the area include the Hampta Pass, Beas Kund, and Bhrigu Lake treks.



Way to Chalal
Chalal, a small village near Kasol in Himachal Pradesh, is a popular destination for trekking enthusiasts. The Chalal Trek is a short and scenic trail that takes you through pine forests, alongside the Parvati River. The trek offers panoramic views of the surrounding mountains and an opportunity to experience the tranquility of nature. Chalal is also known for its hippie culture, making it an ideal destination for backpackers and nature lovers alike.



Way to Chadar
The Chadar Trek, also known as the Frozen River Trek, is a challenging and unique winter trekking experience in Ladakh, India. It involves walking on the frozen Zanskar River, which transforms into a thick sheet of ice during the winter months. Trekkers navigate through narrow gorges, frozen waterfalls, and icy cliffs, surrounded by breathtaking landscapes.
